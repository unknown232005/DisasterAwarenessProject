Landslide

Soil, rocks, and debris moving down a slope due to heavy rain or earthquakes.

About Landslides

Landslides occur when unstable soil or rocks move downhill due to gravity, often triggered by heavy rainfall, earthquakes, or human activity. They can bury roads, houses, and farmland.

How to Prepare

  • Monitor slopes around your home for cracks or unusual movement.
  • Plant vegetation to stabilize soil.
  • Avoid building on steep or unstable slopes.

How to Survive

If a landslide occurs, move quickly out of the path of moving debris. Listen for warning signs like rumbling sounds or sudden water flow changes.

Tips & Tricks

  • Have an emergency escape route planned.
  • Stay alert during heavy rain.
